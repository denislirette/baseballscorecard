Advanced Stats Reference

Advanced Stats Reference

Stats used on BaseballScorecard.org, why they matter, and how they're calculated.

Batting Stats

StatWhy It's UsedFormula
AVG The most traditional measure of hitting ability; shows how often a batter gets a hit. \(\displaystyle \frac{H}{AB}\)
OBP Measures how often a batter reaches base, including walks and HBPs. Getting on base is the most important thing a batter can do. \(\displaystyle \frac{H + BB + HBP}{AB + BB + HBP + SF}\)
SLG Measures raw power by weighting extra-base hits. A home run counts more than a single. \(\displaystyle \frac{1B + 2 \cdot 2B + 3 \cdot 3B + 4 \cdot HR}{AB}\)
OPS Quick combined measure of getting on base and hitting for power. Simple and widely used. \(OBP + SLG\)
wOBA Weights each offensive event by its actual run value using linear weights. More accurate than OPS because it properly values walks vs. doubles vs. home runs. \(\displaystyle \frac{0.696 \cdot uBB + 0.726 \cdot HBP + 0.883 \cdot 1B + 1.244 \cdot 2B + 1.569 \cdot 3B + 2.004 \cdot HR}{AB + BB - IBB + SF + HBP}\)
wRC+ Park- and league-adjusted version of wOBA scaled to 100. A wRC+ of 120 means 20% better than league average. The gold standard for comparing hitters across eras and ballparks. \(\displaystyle \frac{\frac{wRAA}{PA} + \frac{R_{lg}}{PA_{lg}}}{\frac{wRC_{lg}}{PA_{lg}}} \times 100\)
BABIP Batting average on balls in play. Helps identify luck vs. skill; extreme values tend to regress to the mean (~.300). \(\displaystyle \frac{H - HR}{AB - K - HR + SF}\)
PA Plate appearances; the denominator for rate stats. More meaningful than AB because it includes walks, HBPs, and sacrifice flies. \(AB + BB + HBP + SF + SH + CI\)

Pitching Stats

StatWhy It's UsedFormula
ERA The traditional measure of pitching effectiveness. Shows earned runs allowed per 9 innings. \(\displaystyle \frac{ER}{IP} \times 9\)
WHIP Measures how many baserunners a pitcher allows per inning. Lower is better; elite pitchers are under 1.00. \(\displaystyle \frac{BB + H}{IP}\)
IP Innings pitched; measures workload. Each out is 1/3 of an inning (e.g., 6.2 = 6 and 2/3 innings). \(\displaystyle \frac{\text{Outs Recorded}}{3}\)
K/9 Strikeouts per 9 innings. Measures a pitcher's ability to miss bats; higher is better. \(\displaystyle \frac{K}{IP} \times 9\)
BB/9 Walks per 9 innings. Measures control; lower is better. \(\displaystyle \frac{BB}{IP} \times 9\)
K/BB Strikeout-to-walk ratio. Combines stuff and control into one number; elite pitchers are above 4.0. \(\displaystyle \frac{K}{BB}\)
FIP Fielding Independent Pitching. Estimates ERA based only on what pitchers control: strikeouts, walks, HBPs, and home runs. \(\displaystyle \frac{13 \cdot HR + 3(BB + HBP) - 2 \cdot K}{IP} + C_{FIP}\)

Notes

wOBA Weights Linear weights are updated annually by FanGraphs based on the run environment. The values shown here are from the 2024 season. Small year-to-year differences have minimal impact.
wRC+ Requires league-average data that is not available from the MLB Stats API. When FanGraphs data is integrated, wRC+ will be displayed alongside wOBA.
\(C_{FIP}\) The FIP constant is typically around 3.10 and is recalculated each season to align FIP with league-average ERA.
Data Source All stats are sourced from the MLB Stats API (statsapi.mlb.com). Advanced stats like wOBA are calculated client-side from the raw components provided by the API.
