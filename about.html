About This Project

Scorekeeping can look intimidating from the outside. I felt that way too. Then I tried it, and it completely changed how I watch baseball.

This site takes live game data and fills in a traditional scorecard automatically. You can follow along while watching or listening to a game, learn how plays get recorded, or use it as a reference alongside your own scorebook. The goal is to make that first step easier and get more people scoring games.

This is not a replacement for keeping score by hand. That's the art. This is a way in.

Scorebook Downloads

Here are a few scorecards I've found to help you get started. Most are free to download and print at home. Your local library will also have printers you can use if you don't have one. I have no affiliation with any of these.

Bob Carpenter Scorebook The one I use. Clean layout with room for detailed pitch tracking. Designed by the Nationals TV broadcaster. Paid, not free. Purchase
Swingley Scorecards Free PDFs in several layouts, including one with pitcher stats and room for 11 innings. Available in single and double-sided versions. Creative Commons licensed. Free PDF
Reisner Scorekeeping A different approach: you score plays in order as they happen, not inning-by-inning across the page. Lots more room to write. Free to download, donations welcome. Free PDF
Baseball Almanac Simple, traditional layout. Good if you just want a clean sheet to print and bring to a game. No frills. Free PDF
